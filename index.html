Is your laptop lagging?

Click below to see what's slowing it down — and how to fix it.

1

Disable unnecessary startup programs

➜

Open Task Manager (Ctrl+Shift+Esc) → Startup tab, and disable apps you don't need running at boot. Fewer startup apps means faster boot and more free memory.

2

Free up disk space

➜

Run Disk Cleanup (Windows) or Storage Management (macOS) to clear temp files, cache, and old downloads. Keep at least 15-20% of your drive free for smooth performance.

3

Scan for malware

➜

Background malware and adware silently eat CPU and memory. Run a full scan with Windows Defender or your antivirus of choice.

4

Update your OS and drivers

➜

Outdated graphics, chipset, and storage drivers can cause slowdowns and stutter. Check Windows Update or your manufacturer's support site regularly.

5

Upgrade to an SSD or add RAM

➜

If you're still on a spinning hard drive, switching to an SSD is the single biggest speed boost you can buy. More RAM helps if you keep many tabs/apps open.

6

Close background apps and browser tabs

➜

Check Task Manager's Processes tab for memory/CPU hogs. Browsers with dozens of tabs are a common silent drain — close what you're not using.

7

Switch to a high-performance power plan

➜

Laptops on "Battery Saver" or "Balanced" mode throttle CPU speed. Switch to "Best Performance" when plugged in for demanding tasks.

8

Restart regularly

➜

Restarting clears memory leaks and stuck processes that build up over days of uptime. A weekly restart keeps things running smoothly.
